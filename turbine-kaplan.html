Precision diagnostics for low-head turbines: Ensuring optimal adjustable blade performance and mitigating cavitation risk.

A History of Adaptability and Flow

The Kaplan turbine, developed by Viktor Kaplan in the early 20th century, revolutionized low-head hydropower. Its defining feature is its **adjustable runner blades**, allowing it to maintain high efficiency across a much broader range of flow rates than fixed-blade designs. This adaptability makes it critical for river projects and plants with seasonal variations.

However, this adaptability introduces complexity. The hydraulic servo systems and the dynamic interaction between the moving blades and guide vanes are primary points of mechanical wear and potential failure. Our integrity checks focus precisely on these intricate moving parts.
