Severe pressure pulsations caused by complex flow fields in pumped-storage power stations significantly threaten operational safety and stability. With ultra-high-head units (above 700 m) and complex conveyance systems becoming common, traditional 1D modeling is obsolete. This analysis demonstrates why a **fully three-dimensional simulation coupling the pipeline and the pump-turbine unit** is essential to eliminate operational uncertainty.

1. The Critical Flaw of 1D Modeling: Bidirectional Coupling

Current simulation practices often use 1D models for the long water conveyance pipelines, simplifying them into basic linear systems. Our comprehensive 3D analysis, coupling the full water conveyance system with the prototype pump-turbine, reveals a fundamental flaw in this approach:

The study reveals a strong **bidirectional coupling** between the flow in the long outlet pipe and the internal flow within the pump-turbine unit. The existence of complex 3D flow structures propagating along the pipeline underscores the necessity of fully coupled 3D simulations.

Ignoring this complex three-dimensional flow within long pipelines‚Äîespecially those with bifurcations or T-junctions‚Äîresults in **significant errors** in predicting the unit's internal flow characteristics and dynamic instability, directly contributing to the **48% dynamic risk factor** we seek to mitigate.

Key Challenges Identified in 3D Modeling:

  • Vortex Generation: Complex 3D vortices arise at structural features like bifurcations and flow impingement points, which cannot be neglected.
  • Pressure Pulsation Propagation: Pulsations generated by Rotor‚ÄìStator Interaction (RSI) in the vaneless space propagate differently than previously assumed, interacting with the entire pipeline.
  • Ultra-High Head Effects: High-fidelity simulations at prototype scale are critical for systems operating above 700 meters, where fluid compressibility must be accurately modeled.

The detailed analysis of pressure pulsations reveals how instability spreads throughout the system:
