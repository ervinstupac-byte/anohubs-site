1. The Electro-Erosion Threat

WARNING: Shaft voltages as low as 1.0V can cause electrical discharge machining (EDM) in bearings.

When grounding is compromised, the path of least resistance for stray currents becomes the thin oil film in the guide bearings. This micro-arcing pits the metal surface, leading to rapid mechanical failure often misdiagnosed as simple "wear and tear."

2. Diagnostic Protocol

Identifying "Ghost Vibrations"

We differentiate between mechanical and electrically-induced vibration:

Symptom Mechanical Cause Electrical Cause (M-E Synergy)
Vibration Frequency 1x RPM (Unbalance) 2x Line Frequency (e.g. 100Hz/120Hz)
Load Dependency Often constant Changes instantly with Excitation
Sound Rumbling / Grinding Magnetic Hum / Drone

3. Mitigation Strategy

  • 1. Shaft Grounding Brushes Installation of silver-graphite brushes to passively bleed static charge from the shaft to ground.
  • 2. Insulated Bearings Mandatory use of NDE (Non-Drive End) bearings with ceramic or polymer insulation to break the circuit.
