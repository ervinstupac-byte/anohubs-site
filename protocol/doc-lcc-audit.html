LCC & Procurement Integrity Veto

Financial and Material Guide for Securing Long-term Asset Value Over Low CAPEX.

1. Executive Summary: The Cost of Cheap Decisions

The pursuit of the lowest capital expenditure (CAPEX) during procurement is a globally recognized source of hidden, long-term financial failure. This protocol mandates a comprehensive **Life Cycle Cost (LCC)** analysis to quantify operational expenditures (OpEx) over a 30-year span, providing the executive team with the critical data needed to veto financially flawed procurement decisions.

KEY VETO MANDATE

All components must be subjected to a material compatibility audit, focusing on areas prone to **Galvanic Corrosion**, to ensure the expected service life is met and LCC is minimized.

2. Material Integrity Audit (Galvanic Corrosion)

Galvanic corrosion—the accelerated degradation of metal due to contact with a less noble metal in an electrolyte (water)—is a prime example of a failure caused by poor procurement decisions. Our audit verifies the following:

2.1. Material Compatibility Index

Components in direct hydraulic contact (runners, guide vanes, seals) must meet a stringent compatibility index based on the water medium (fresh, brackish, or salt water). Suboptimal mixing of steel, brass, and bronze alloys is strictly prohibited.

2.2. LCC Modeling Criteria

The financial model must prove that maintenance savings over 15 years exceed the initial cost difference (CAPEX delta). Key OpEx parameters quantified include:

  • Projected downtime costs (lost revenue).
  • Estimated replacement frequency of critical components (bearings, seals).
  • Labor costs associated with unscheduled maintenance.

3. Mitigation Strategy

The audit concludes with a clear recommendation to procurement leadership:

  1. **Material Veto:** If material certifications do not meet the AnoHUB standard, the procurement must be vetoed, regardless of the lower CAPEX price.
  2. **Long-term Coating:** Mandate specialized protective coatings (e.g., ceramic-metal composites) on high-stress hydraulic surfaces to extend service life and justify the LCC investment.
