Precision Alignment Protocol: The 0.05 mm Mandate

Detailed Technical Standard for Shaft Alignment, Vibration, and Concentricity Verification.

1. Executive Summary: Eliminating the 48% Risk

This document provides the technical specifications and procedures for the execution of turbine-generator **shaft alignment** and **concentricity checks**. The purpose is to eliminate the primary mechanical flaw arising from the **Organizational Execution Gap**, which is proven to be the root cause of the latent **48% dynamic instability risk** in rotating machinery. Compliance with this protocol guarantees the unit is hydrodynamically and mechanically balanced from day one.

KEY TECHNICAL MANDATE

All alignment procedures must achieve and verify a tolerance of **0.05 mm per meter of shaft span**. Failure to comply with this standard invalidates the guarantee of Engineering Immunity against dynamic risks.

2.2. Post-Assembly Validation

Before final acceptance, dynamic testing must confirm the elimination of unstable harmonics:

  • **Vibration Spectrum Analysis:** Verification that vibration levels are below ISO 10816 standards, with no dominant frequencies aligning with calculated critical speeds.
  • **Thermal Stability:** Monitoring bearing temperatures across the load range to ensure adequate lubrication and stress distribution.
