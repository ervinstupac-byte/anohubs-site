Precision Alignment Protocol

REF: DOC-ALIGN-005 | REV: 2.0 | STATUS: MANDATORY

1. Executive Summary & Mandate

Dynamic instability accounts for nearly 48% of premature failures in vertical hydro units. This protocol defines the mandatory specifications for turbine-generator shaft alignment. It is not a guideline; it is a rigid standard.

CRITICAL TOLERANCE:

Total shaft runout must not exceed 0.05 mm per meter (0.002 in/ft) of shaft length. Exceeding this voids the operational guarantee.

2. Pre-Alignment Prerequisites

Before laser measurement begins, the following physical conditions must be verified to prevent "False Good" readings:

1
Soft Foot Check: Loosen each hold-down bolt individually. If shaft movement > 0.03mm is detected, the baseplate foundation is compromised and must be shimmed/grouted before alignment proceeds.
2
Thermal Growth Calculation: Alignment targets must be offset to account for operational temperature expansion (ΔT). Cold alignment targets are calculated using the coefficient of expansion for the specific shaft steel grade.
3
Bearing Clearance: Guide bearing clearances must be measured and centered prior to shaft coupling.

3. Methodology & ISO Tolerances

We utilize dual-laser measurement systems. The following table outlines the AnoHUB standard compared to generic ISO 10816 limits:
