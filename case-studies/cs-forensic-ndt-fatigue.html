Material Integrity: Forensic NDT Stops Early Fatigue

**Client Profile:** High-stress Francis Turbine | **Problem:** Invisible Micro-Cracking | **Solution:** Phased Array UT | **Outcome:** Catastrophe Avoided.

1. The Challenge: Invisible Threats

During a routine inspection cycle of a 150MW Francis unit, site engineers suspected material degradation in the high-stress zones of the runner blades (near the root and trailing edges). However, traditional visual inspections and standard dye penetrant tests failed to confirm the extent of the damage.

The unit was operating under a **"blind" risk** of blade separation due to Stress Corrosion Cracking (SCC), which propagates internally before breaking the surface.

2. The AnoHUB Protocol: Looking Beneath the Surface

  • Advanced Ultrasonic Testing (PAUT): We utilized Phased Array Ultrasonic Testing to scan deep into the blade structure, detecting sub-surface flaws and measuring the exact depth of crack initiation with 0.5mm accuracy.
  • Magnetic Particle Inspection (MPI): Applied fluorescent MPI under UV light to identify surface-breaking micro-fractures in ferromagnetic stress zones that were invisible to the naked eye.
  • Material Analysis & Repair Blueprint: We mapped the fatigue zones and provided a precise welding and heat-treatment blueprint to arrest crack growth without compromising the runner's hydraulic profile.
