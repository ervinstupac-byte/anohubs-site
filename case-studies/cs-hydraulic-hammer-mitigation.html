Pressure Pulse Control: Eliminating Hydraulic Hammer

**Problem:** Dangerous Pressure Spikes | **Solution:** Governor Recalibration | **Outcome:** 40% Surge Reduction.

1. The Challenge: Structural Threat

A high-head pumped storage unit (600m+) suffered catastrophic pressure spikes during emergency trips and load rejections. These pressure transients (water hammer) threatened the structural integrity of the penstock and increased vibration across the entire powerhouse, leading to major safety concerns and regulatory non-compliance.

The hidden risk was an **outdated governor closing law** that did not account for the complex inertia of the long water column, causing rapid flow stoppage and massive pressure rebound.

2. The AnoHUB Protocol: Mathematical Modeling

  • Transient 3D Simulation: Conducted a high-fidelity dynamic simulation of the entire water conveyance system to map pressure wave propagation and reflection points.
  • Governor Recalibration: Implemented a new, multi-stage closing law (slow-fast-slow) derived from the simulation to absorb transient energy gradually without stalling the unit.
  • Safety Validation: Verified the final pressure surge against international safety standards and the penstock's original design limits during live load rejection tests.
