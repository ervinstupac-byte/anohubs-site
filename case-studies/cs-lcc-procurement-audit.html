Procurement Integrity: LCC Audit Secures 30-Year Value

**Client Profile:** Large Public Utility, Pre-Modernization Phase | **Challenge Duration:** Pre-contract risk assessment | **AnoHUB Intervention:** Q3 2025.

1. The Challenge: Low-Bid Trap and Hidden Material Risk

The client was about to sign a contract with the lowest bidder for a critical component overhaul. Our pre-contract audit revealed that the low CapEx price required the manufacturer to use suboptimal materials and coatings, specifically mixing incompatible metals. This posed a high risk of accelerated **Galvanic Corrosion** and premature failure, massively inflating the Life Cycle Cost (LCC) post-installation.

  • **Key Symptom:** Financial pressure to accept the cheapest upfront price, ignoring OpEx penalties.
  • **Hidden Risk:** Suboptimal material selection that would guarantee component failure within 5-7 years, requiring unscheduled replacements.

2. The AnoHUB Protocol: LCC Audit and Integrity Veto

We deployed a financial and material science audit before the contract was finalized:

  1. **LCC Modeling:** Developed a verifiable financial model demonstrating that the "low bid" would result in **18% higher costs** over 30 years due to inevitable maintenance and downtime.
  2. **Material Compatibility Audit:** Identified the precise risk of galvanic corrosion linked to the proposed material mixing.
  3. **Procurement Veto:** Provided the executive team with irrefutable data, allowing them to veto the low-bid contract in favor of a provider guaranteeing long-term material integrity.
