Targeted analysis for medium-head turbines: Focusing on hydraulic stability, cavitation risk, and component longevity.

The Francis Challenge: Pressure and Flow Dynamics

The Francis turbine, ideal for medium-head applications, operates under immense hydraulic pressure where efficiency hinges on maintaining perfect flow over the complex runner geometry. Our specialized audits ensure that this geometry is optimal and that pressure differentials do not lead to localized structural failure.

Its setup—where water enters the runner radially and exits axially—is what gives it its unique, powerful hydraulic signature. For us, maintaining the integrity of this design is paramount, as every fraction of efficiency contributes directly to sustainable power generation.

Focus Protocol: Maximizing Lifespan

We address the specific vulnerabilities of Francis runners and guide vanes:

  • CFD Optimization:

    Utilizing Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) to validate and adjust blade profiles and flow paths, ensuring maximum hydraulic efficiency and reducing hydraulic hammer risks.

  • Acoustic Emission Monitoring (AEM):

    Non-invasive acoustic monitoring to detect the precise onset and location of cavitation, allowing for preemptive intervention and repair before damage escalates.

  • Partial Load Instability:

    Auditing turbine performance during off-design (partial load) operation, where flow separation and resulting vibrations pose the greatest long-term threat.

  • Rotor-Stator Interaction (RSI) Mitigation:

    Detailed analysis of the clearances and hydraulic forces between the runner and guide vanes to minimize dynamic excitation and noise generation.
